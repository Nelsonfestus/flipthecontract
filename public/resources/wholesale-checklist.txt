===============================================
  FLIP THE CONTRACT — WHOLESALE DEAL CHECKLIST
  Step-by-Step Guide from Lead to Closing
===============================================

This checklist walks you through every step of a wholesale real estate deal.
Print it, save it, and use it on every deal until it becomes second nature.


PHASE 1: FINDING THE DEAL
--------------------------
[ ] Identify your target market (city, zip codes, neighborhoods)
[ ] Pull a motivated seller list (probate, pre-foreclosure, tax delinquent, absentee owners, vacant properties)
[ ] Skip trace the list for owner phone numbers and emails
[ ] Begin outreach campaign:
    [ ] Cold calling (use scripts from FTC)
    [ ] Direct mail (use marketing templates from FTC)
    [ ] SMS/text campaigns (follow TCPA rules)
    [ ] Driving for dollars
[ ] Track all leads in your CRM
[ ] Follow up with every lead at least 5 times


PHASE 2: ANALYZING THE DEAL
-----------------------------
[ ] Get the property address and owner info
[ ] Pull comparable sales (comps) — look at sold properties within 0.5 miles, last 6 months
[ ] Determine After Repair Value (ARV)
[ ] Estimate repair costs (get contractor bids if possible)
[ ] Calculate Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO):
    MAO = ARV x 0.70 - Repair Costs - Your Assignment Fee
[ ] Verify there are no major title issues (liens, judgments, code violations)
[ ] Confirm the seller's motivation and timeline


PHASE 3: LOCKING UP THE CONTRACT
----------------------------------
[ ] Present your offer to the seller (verbal first, then written)
[ ] Use the correct contract for your state (see FTC Contract Templates)
[ ] Key contract clauses to include:
    [ ] "And/or assigns" after your name (allows assignment)
    [ ] Inspection contingency (gives you an exit)
    [ ] Earnest money terms (keep it low — $10 to $100 for motivated sellers)
    [ ] Closing date (30-45 days gives you time to find a buyer)
[ ] Get the contract signed by all parties
[ ] Deliver earnest money to the title company or escrow
[ ] Open escrow with an investor-friendly title company


PHASE 4: FINDING A BUYER (DISPOSITION)
----------------------------------------
[ ] Blast the deal to your buyer list (use FTC Disposition Email Templates)
[ ] Post in local real estate investor Facebook groups
[ ] List on real estate investor marketplaces
[ ] Contact hedge fund buyers and investment brokerages (see FTC Buyer Lists)
[ ] Include all key details:
    [ ] Property address
    [ ] ARV and comps
    [ ] Repair estimate
    [ ] Assignment price
    [ ] Photos (exterior + interior if available)
[ ] Respond to buyer inquiries within 1 hour
[ ] Schedule property showings or provide lockbox access
[ ] Collect proof of funds (POF) from serious buyers


PHASE 5: ASSIGNING THE CONTRACT
---------------------------------
[ ] Negotiate your assignment fee with the buyer
[ ] Prepare the Assignment of Contract (see FTC templates)
[ ] Collect a non-refundable earnest money deposit from the buyer ($2,000-$5,000+)
[ ] Send the signed assignment to the title company
[ ] Provide the title company with:
    [ ] Original purchase contract
    [ ] Assignment of contract
    [ ] Buyer's earnest money
    [ ] Buyer's proof of funds


PHASE 6: CLOSING THE DEAL
----------------------------
[ ] Stay in contact with the title company weekly
[ ] Confirm clear title and no surprises
[ ] Coordinate with the seller for any needed access or documents
[ ] Confirm buyer's funding is ready
[ ] Review the settlement statement (HUD-1 or closing disclosure)
[ ] Verify your assignment fee is correct on the closing docs
[ ] Attend closing (or arrange for mobile notary if remote)
[ ] Collect your check!


PHASE 7: POST-CLOSE
---------------------
[ ] Add the buyer to your repeat buyer list with notes on their preferences
[ ] Follow up with the seller — ask for referrals
[ ] Document the deal for your records (purchase price, ARV, fees, timeline)
[ ] Celebrate the win and share in the FTC Community!
[ ] Start finding your next deal


=== QUICK REFERENCE: KEY FORMULAS ===

Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO):
  ARV x 0.70 - Repairs - Assignment Fee = MAO

Assignment Fee Rule of Thumb:
  $5,000 - $15,000 for single family
  $10,000 - $50,000+ for multi-family or commercial

Comp Selection Criteria:
  - Same property type (SFR, multi-family, land)
  - Within 0.5 miles (1 mile max in rural areas)
  - Sold within last 6 months (12 months max)
  - Similar bed/bath count and square footage
  - Similar condition and lot size


=== IMPORTANT REMINDERS ===

- Always use state-specific contracts (see FTC Contracts & Legal section)
- Never use your personal funds for earnest money — use an LLC or trust
- Build relationships with 2-3 investor-friendly title companies
- Your buyer list is your most valuable asset — grow it daily
- Follow up is where the money is — most deals close after the 3rd-5th contact
- Stay compliant with your state's wholesaling laws
